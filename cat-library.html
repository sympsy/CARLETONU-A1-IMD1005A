Some Orange Cats

A Formatted Poem About Cats

A creature of shadows, a whisper of night,
With eyes like emeralds, gleaming bright.
A hunter's grace, a silent tread,
Where shadows dance and dreams are bred.

She glides through the house, a phantom unseen,
A symphony of purrs, a gentle queen.
A flick of the tail, a playful swat,
A mischievous glint, a knowing cat.

She curls in a ball, a furry embrace,
A comforting warmth, a peaceful space.
A purring engine, a rhythmic hum,
As shadows lengthen, and darkness comes.

She watches the world with knowing eyes,
A silent observer, beneath the skies.
A creature of mystery, a timeless art,
Forever entwined with the human heart.

From ancient Egypt to modern day,
The cat has held our hearts in sway.
A loyal companion, a friend so true,
The enigmatic feline, forever anew.

So let us cherish these creatures of grace,
With their playful spirit and gentle face.
For in their presence, we find delight,
A touch of magic, both day and night.
